About EcoLoop

Founded February 2026 by five Syracuse students—reimagining air filtration as something that fits real apartments and dorms, not only utility closets.

Mission statement. EcoLoop seeks to reinvent the indoor space. Ensuring a clean and safe living environment shouldn’t have to be actively achieved, but rather automated and guaranteed.

Our story

EcoLoop was established in February 2026 by five Syracuse students aiming to seamlessly integrate air filtration into home environments for self-sustaining clean air. Frustrated by bulky, unattractive, and high-maintenance air purification systems, the team set out to reimagine air filtration as both functional and aesthetically pleasing.

The idea for EcoLoop emerged from personal experience and market research. Living in shared apartments and dorms, the founders noticed that traditional air purifiers were often single-purpose and did not complement their living spaces. Research indicated that placing an air purifier in a corner can reduce its coverage area by up to 50% due to blocked airflow. This obstruction creates dead zones where polluted air stagnates and does not reach the filters.

That insight inspired a design-focused plant pot that integrates air purification and dehumidification. The pot features a small, fist-sized, two-chamber system that converts particle waste into hydrogen gas, allowing it to operate independently of external sources. It automates plant care by collecting, storing, and releasing water from the dehumidifier as needed. EcoLoop enhances indoor air quality, reduces maintenance, and blends into any living space.
