The EcoLoop planter

Built for the cramped reality of urban apartments and dorms, EcoLoop replaces bulky standalone hardware with a single planter that cleans air, manages moisture, and fits naturally into your space.

Why it exists

In shoebox apartments, opening a window is rarely enough to deal with dust, pollutants, and allergens. Traditional purifiers take up valuable room and can lose up to 50% of effective airflow when pushed into corners.

Most students and early-career renters do not have the space for a separate purifier, dehumidifier, and plant care setup, so healthy living starts to feel like extra clutter.

EcoLoop exists to combine those jobs into one decor-first planter that stays in active parts of the room, improves circulation where people actually live, and makes cleaner air feel effortless instead of another appliance to hide.

How it works

A built-in dehumidifier pulls moisture from the air and stores it to automatically irrigate the plant. HEPA filtration captures airborne particles, and a selective membrane chamber converts collected waste into hydrogen gas to help power the system.

  • One object replaces separate purifier, dehumidifier, and extra floor clutter
  • Designed for active placement, not corner dead zones with blocked airflow
  • Built-in watering loop: air moisture -> storage -> plant soil
  • HEPA capture plus membrane conversion of collected waste into hydrogen gas

Components

Each component is designed to do a specific job in the EcoLoop system and reduce the need for separate appliances in small spaces.
